1. Introduction to Ulcerative Colitis#

Ulcerative Colitis is a chronic disease which causes inflammation in the large intestine. It is one of the two diseases that make up Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD). Symptoms of ulcerative colitis may include abdominal pain, cramping, and diarrhea. Although there is no known cure for the disease, certain dietary changes can help manage symptoms. Adding anti-inflammatory foods to your diet can help reduce inflammation and may reduce pain and discomfort associated with the condition. Here are five must-have foods for an anti-inflammatory diet in people with ulcerative colitis.

First, researchers have found that fatty fish such as salmon, mackerel, herring, and sardines contain high levels of omega-3 essential fatty acids, which may reduce inflammation. It’s important to include these types of fish to your diet two to three times a week.

Secondly, nuts and seeds are a great source of fiber, as well as vitamin E and healthy fats. Walnuts, flaxseeds, and chia seeds are especially helpful, as they contain high levels of omega-3 fatty acids. Aim to include a handful of nuts and seeds to your diet each day.

Thirdly, garlic and onions contain compounds that can reduce inflammation in the body. Adding garlic and onion to your meals on a daily basis can help reduce pain and inflammation.

Fourth, fruits, especially berries, are packed with antioxidants which fight inflammation. Berries such as blackberries, blueberries, raspberries, and strawberries are especially helpful in reducing inflammation.

Finally, ginger is great for reducing inflammation and relieving pain. It can be added to meals or steeped in hot water to make a tea.

Incorporating these five must-have anti-inflammatory foods to your diet can help reduce inflammation and pain associated with ulcerative colitis. Speak with a healthcare provider for more information on how to manage ulcerative colitis with diet.
