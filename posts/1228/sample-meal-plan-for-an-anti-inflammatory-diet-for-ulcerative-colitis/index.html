Ulcerative colitis is a type of inflammatory bowel disease in which the lining of the large intestine becomes inflamed and ulcerated. An anti-inflammatory diet can help reduce symptoms of the condition. This article provides a sample meal plan for an anti-inflammatory diet for ulcerative colitis.

Breakfast can include a bowl of oatmeal with fruit, nuts, and seeds. Healthy fats such as extra-virgin olive oil or coconut oil can be added for extra flavor.

Lunch can be a salad with mixed greens, nuts, seeds, and grilled chicken. For a more filling meal, add some whole grain crackers and hummus.

For dinner, a salmon fillet with quinoa or brown rice and steamed vegetables make a healthy and tasty meal.

Q. What foods should I include in an anti-inflammatory diet? A. Foods that are typically included in an anti-inflammatory diet are fresh fruits and vegetables, oily fish like salmon, nuts and seeds, whole grains, legumes, and healthy fats like olive oil.
