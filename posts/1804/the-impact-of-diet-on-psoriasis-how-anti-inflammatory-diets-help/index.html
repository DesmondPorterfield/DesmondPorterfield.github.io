Psoriasis is an inflammatory skin condition that is estimated to affect up to seven million Americans. It is generally believed to be triggered by an overactive immune system, and can range in severity from minor, localized patches to extensive body coverage. A key factor in the development of psoriasis is diet, and certain anti-inflammatory foods can help to treat the condition in many patients.

Inflammatory diets contain higher levels of saturated fats, simple carbohydrates, and trans-fats, which have all been linked to an increase in inflammation in the body. Many studies have shown that shrimp, dairy, and red meat can irritate psoriasis symptoms. Eating a diet with more anti-inflammatory foods, such as fatty fish, nuts, and olive oil, may taken to reduce psoriasis symptoms.

Eating more fiber is also believed to be beneficial, and can be found in whole grains like quinoa and oats.

Although there is no cure for psoriasis, the right diet can help to reduce symptoms and manage the condition. Eating a diet rich in anti-inflammatory foods can help to reduce inflammation, and may help to improve overall skin health. If you have psoriasis, it is important to talk to your doctor or a nutritionist to help create an anti-inflammatory diet that can help reduce symptoms of the condition.
