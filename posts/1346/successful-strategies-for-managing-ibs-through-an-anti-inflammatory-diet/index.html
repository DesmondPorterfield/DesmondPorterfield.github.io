Eating this way can help to balance the immune system and reduce inflammation. Additionally, an anti-inflammatory diet can reduce symptoms of IBS by eliminating trigger foods such as dairy, processed foods, and caffeine.

The most important part of an anti-inflammatory diet, is to focus on nutrient-dense whole foods, such as fruits and vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, nuts, and seeds. Overall, an anti-inflammatory diet can be an effective way to manage IBS symptoms. While there is no one-size-fits-all diet for IBS, following an anti-inflammatory diet that focuses on whole, nutrient-dense foods, and avoiding trigger foods, can help to reduce IBS symptoms and lead to improved digestive health.
