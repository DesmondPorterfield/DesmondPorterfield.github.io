II. Strategies for Making Sustainable Diet Changes#

Making sustainable diet changes is a key part of managing endometriosis. Eating nutritious meals with a healthful balance of vitamins, minerals, and essential fatty acids can reduce inflammation, provide needed energy, and improve overall wellbeing. Here are some strategies for making sustainable diet changes:

First, create an action plan and establish realistic goals. Kicking off a new diet can be overwhelming, so many experts recommend starting with small changes. Write out your goals and create an actionable plan with timelines.

Second, identify triggers that lead to unhealthy eating. Keeping a food log and paying attention to physical and emotional responses to certain foods can help identify potential triggers. Removing those triggers can make it easier to stay on track with a healthful diet.

Third, build a support network. Family and friends can be powerful sources of support when making major life changes. Tell them why you’re making changes and ask for their help in staying accountable.

Finally, solicit help from a nutritionist or dietician. A professional can help to provide a healthy meal plan and customize a program to address the individual needs of the person managing endometriosis. Building a personalized plan can help to ensure that diet changes are sustainable in the long term.

Third, be mindful of ingredients. Knowing what ingredients are in the foods you’re eating can help you make healthier choices overall. Limiting processed foods, sugar, and refined grains can help reduce inflammation.
