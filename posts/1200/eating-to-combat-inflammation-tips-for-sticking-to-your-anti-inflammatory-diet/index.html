1. Understand the Causes of Inflammation#

Eating to Combat Inflammation: Tips for Sticking to Your Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Inflammation is a natural response from the body to protect us against harmful and foreign substances. Understanding the causes of inflammation can help us understand what we should do to reduce it. Here are some tips for sticking to an anti-inflammatory diet.

Eat fewer processed foods that contain artificial preservatives, food colorings, and trans fats. Replace these with fresh or frozen vegetables, fruits, lean cuts of meat, legumes, nuts, and whole grains. Make sure to get enough fiber and essential fatty acids from these foods too.

Consume anti-inflammatory foods like turmeric, ginger, garlic, and omega-3 fatty acids found in cold-water fish, walnuts, and flaxseed oil. These will reduce inflammation and can be incorporated into meals in many different ways.

Make sure to watch portion sizes. Eating more than needed can lead to weight gain, which can increase inflammation. Choose smaller portion sizes of healthy foods to minimize overeating and stay on a healthy path to reduce inflammation.

Stay hydrated. Water aids in digestion and helps to flush out toxins that may be contributing to inflammation. Try to drink 8-10 glasses of water a day for optimal health.

2. Select the Right Foods#

Eating to combat inflammation starts with selecting the right foods. The best anti-inflammatory diet consists of foods that are high in antioxidants and low in saturated fats. Fruits like blueberries, oranges, and strawberries, as well as leafy greens like spinach and kale, are good sources of antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation. Eating lean proteins like fish, chicken, and turkey can also help reduce inflammation, as protein helps the body to repair and build new cells. Eating fatty fish like salmon or tuna is another good option for an anti-inflammatory diet as these fish are high in omega-3 fatty acids. Additionally, avoiding processed foods and limiting sugar intake can help reduce inflammation.

1. Building an Anti-Inflammatory Diet#

Eating an anti-inflammatory diet is a great way to reduce inflammation and improve overall health. The key to any diet is consistency, and sticking to an anti-inflammatory diet is no different. Here are some tips to help you stay on track with an anti-inflammatory diet.

First, plan ahead and prep food. Meal prepping is a great way to ensure you have nutritious meals ready when hunger strikes. Taking the time to plan out your meals and snacks helps keep you focused and eliminates the temptation of unhealthy options.

Second, add in anti-inflammatory spices and herbs. Adding spices like turmeric, ginger, and garlic to dishes not only boosts flavor but has been known to help reduce inflammation. Plus, they can help reduce the amount of salt and oil that is added to your meals.

Third, fill half of your plate with fruits and vegetables. Eating plenty of vegetables and fruits is essential for any diet, but is especially important for an anti-inflammatory diet. Fruits and vegetables are full of antioxidants and other inflammation-fighting phytonutrients.

Finally, cut down on processed and packaged foods. Eating whole, unprocessed foods is the best way to avoid inflammatory ingredients. Try to reach for fresh, organic options whenever possible.

2. Maintaining an Anti-Inflammatory Diet#

An anti-inflammatory diet is a great way to promote overall health and wellbeing while reducing inflammation and the risk of chronic illnesses. To ensure success, it’s important to plan ahead and have a good understanding of the foods you should be consuming. Here are some tips for establishing and maintaining an anti-inflammatory diet.

Plan Ahead - Start out by preparing meals in advance. This will ensure that you always have easy access to healthy and nutritious meals, and you won’t have to rely on unhealthy convenience foods.

Be organised - Stock your kitchen with the right foods and make sure to always have a variety of fresh fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, healthy fats and whole grains to choose from. That way, you won’t be caught without anything nutritious when cravings hit.

Stay Hydrated - Staying hydrated is key to maintaining a healthy diet, so make sure to drink plenty of water throughout the day. Including fresh fruits and vegetables in your diet can also help you stay hydrated, as they contain a significant amount of water.

Nourish Your Body - Try to eat foods that are nutrient-dense and promote health. Choose healthy fats such as nuts, avocados, and olive oils, and limit the quantity of unhealthy fats such as deep-fried foods and processed snacks. Eating nutrient-dense foods will ensure you are nourishing your body with the vitamins and minerals it needs.
